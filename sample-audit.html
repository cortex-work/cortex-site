What it is

Cortex Work helps fast-moving teams understand how decisions, rationale, ownership, commitments, and change signals get lost across daily work.

The Sample Audit is a small, scoped review of selected work conversations or documents. It is designed to show whether meaningful organizational memory can be surfaced from a limited set of real company material before any broader pilot or integration.

What we ask for
  • 3–10 Slack / Teams threads
  • meeting notes from a project or roadmap discussion
  • decision-heavy email chains
  • project updates where direction changed
  • postmortem / incident follow-up discussions
  • planning conversations with ownership or commitment signals

No full workspace rollout is required for the sample audit.

What Cortex Work returns
  • decision candidates
  • rationale behind decisions
  • ownership signals
  • commitments and follow-ups
  • uncertainty or review-needed areas
  • possible change or conflict signals
  • source references for each surfaced item

The output is not treated as automatic truth. It is reviewable and source-linked.

What this is not
  • a full deployment
  • a permanent connector installation
  • a training commitment
  • a black-box AI judgment over company data
  • a generic meeting summary

The goal is to understand whether Cortex Work can surface useful organizational memory from a small, controlled sample.

Ideal first use cases
  • Why did we change this direction?
  • Who owned this decision?
  • Was this actually decided or only proposed?
  • What did we commit to?
  • Did this later conversation change the original decision?
  • Are we remembering this decision correctly?
Expected outcome

At the end of the sample audit, the team receives a compact readout showing what Cortex Work could reliably surface, where the source evidence is strong, and where human review is still needed.

The goal is to decide whether a broader pilot is worth exploring.
